Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

Within the next 10 years I would like to be a project manager in web development, preferably in the consultancy space of the industry.

Strengths when it comes to learning

  • I'm curious, so I ask a lot of questions.
  • If I run into a problem I am able to troubleshoot it either by searching for the correct answer, or by trial and error.
  • I am adaptive, open to taking on new ideas and constructive feedback.
  • I'm analytical and process orientated, which so far seems to be a bonus for developers.

Limitations when it comes to learning

  • Web dev is very detailed oriented so I'm stretching some atrophied muscles.
  • I can become too focussed on a particular thing and spend too much time on it.
  • I can be very hard on myself.

What skills (non-technical core/human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I'd like to continue to grow my communication skills. How we frame things and pose them to a group can be half the battle sometimes; on a bad day I can be pretty blunt and it's not a communication quality that works amazingly well in most environments.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, Facilitators and industry/community representatives:

I will work to communicate openly with other learners, and facilitators to make sure we are all on the same page about what we are doing, and how. I think it's also important to know your team mates - they might have something going on in their lives that may be holding them up for a minute, so getting to know them will let us all show each other some patience and grace.

What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you:

Excercise! Take a break, go for a run or go to the gym. Basically do something that forces me to breathe properly.

A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

If I've gone through the course material, googled the query, looked through the forums for anyone asking the same question - then I will ask for help. I don't want to be a “help vampire” but I also don't plan on wasting too much time.

A description of what you expect from the Facilitation team

Open, constructive feedback and communication. I need to know what I'm doing well, and what I need to work on to get better.
