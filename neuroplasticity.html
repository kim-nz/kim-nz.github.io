Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

Neuroplasticity allows your brain to adapt and change, promoting:
  • The ability to learn new things
  • Enhanced cognitive capabilities
  • Recovery from traumatic brain injuries
  • Strengthening areas where function is lost or has declined
Understanding the principles of neuroplasticity helps us realised the brain is plastic and malleable - we aren’t limited by what we can currently do, we have the ability to get better.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.

Practice, practice, practice. Practice will make those neural pathways stronger over time. Whether is learning JavaScript, or learning a musical instrument, practice will build neural connections.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

Apart from all the new Dev Academy material I'm learning and engaging with on a daily basis, I'm also relearning how to play Piano, and learning how to play Golf. All of these activities require regular practice. Puzzles are a great way to keep the brain engaged, too. Crosswords and sudokus are my favorite kind of puzzles and I try to do a few every week. This boils down to enriching my environment with activities that require me to focus, and are a challenge.
Getting good, quality rest is also important. It makes it easier to concentrate - as does exercise! Exercise clears your mind and helps you focus.

What is a Growth Mindset and why is it relevant to us

A growth mindset is a belief that your abilities are not set in stone - that intelligence and talent can be developed over time. This is really relevant for us as we go through the Dev Academy program. We are learning a lot and I for one am not immediately fluent at Javascript, for example. However, with a growth mindset I know that with work over time I can improve my skills.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I had a slow dawning realisation that in the past I've tricked myself into believing that just because I'm at something immediately I would never be good at it.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

When something in the course material feels difficult I just need to keep in mind that with work, I'll get better at this over time. I'm pretty good at asking questions when I need to, but what I learnt last week was that I shouldn't let myself get to the point of failure before asking for help. With the amount of new material that's being thrown at us, it isn't a weakness or a sign of failure to ask for help when you need it. And the sooner you ask, the less time you waste.
